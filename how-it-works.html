Under the hood

How the Election Engine Works

The simulator uses weighted probability, not pure randomness, so results are believable, replayable, and never identical twice.

  1. 01
    Trait Score. Each candidate's eight traits (charisma, integrity, public speaking, debate skill, fundraising, popularity, experience, intelligence) are combined into a weighted score out of 100.
  2. 02
    National Mood. The engine rolls a random ideological "mood" for each of the eight policy issues, then measures how closely each candidate's platform matches that mood.
  3. 03
    Campaign Events. Between 3 and 6 events are drawn from a pool of 16 — debates, scandals, endorsements, economic swings, and more — each nudging one candidate's support up or down.
  4. 04
    State-by-State Simulation. All 51 electoral-map entries (50 states plus D.C.) are simulated individually. Home-state favorite bonuses, regional policy-focus matches, swing-state volatility, and independent random noise all apply per state.
  5. 05
    Electoral & Popular Vote. Electors are tallied per state (538 total, 270 to win) and the national popular vote is calculated as a turnout-weighted average across every state.
  6. 06
    Results Breakdown. The winner's top traits, the deciding issues, and the single most influential campaign event are surfaced, along with a campaign rating from "Nail-Biter" to "Historic Landslide."

Never Identical

National mood, campaign events, and per-state noise are re-rolled every run, so no two elections play out the same way — even with identical candidates.

Weighted, Not Random

Stronger candidates on the issues that matter to the national mood are favored — but a well-timed event can still flip a close race.

Everything Local

All of this runs client-side in your browser using plain JavaScript — nothing is sent to a server, ever.
